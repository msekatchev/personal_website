Vacuum Traineeship at ITER Vacuum Division

Diagram of the Vacuum System created during the traineeship
Conflat Flange assembled during the traineeship
In the summer of 2016, I completed my work experience at the Vacuum Division at ITER, France.

I recieved hands on experience working with Vacuum systems, and saw how a large international project was organized and coordinated.
  • I spent a lot of my traineeship at the Iter Vacuum Lab, learning and using top of the line Vacuum equipment.
  • I learnt new skills and gained knowledge in operating and maintaining Vacuum systems and Vacuum Equipment, such as:
    • Clean assembly of Vacuum flanges and their function and efficiency in Vacuum systems
    • Setting up and operating a Vacuum system
    • Performing Helium leak detection on a Vacuum system, using a MSLD (Mass Spectrometer Leak Detector)
    • Analysing output data from a MSLD
    • Using a Thermal Outgassing Rig and a Residual Gas Analyser to measure the outgassing rate of different materials.
    • Analysing a RGA graph (clean system vs contaminated system).
    • Viewing and editing the Vacuum Materials Database (Understanding different grades of materials, and the criteria using which they are organised/approved)
    • Understanding the use of liquid dye penetrants to locate surface defects on materials
    • Learning about other methods of sealing Vacuum Flanges.
    • Learning about the Vacuum System inside ITER, one of the largest vacuum systems ever built.
  • Aside from Vacuum-related activities, I also did the following:
    • Go on a virtual tour of the ITER Tokamak
    • Learn about 3D modelling using Catia software
    • Participate in a Vacuum Team Meeting with a group in Germany
    • Go on a tour of the ITER Construction site, whilst learning about the construction work and machinery required to assemble the Tokamak.
    • Perform a presentation about everything that I learned throughout the course. You can download and view this presentation by clicking here.
